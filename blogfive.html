In this essay, I will be discussing the relationship between data visualization, data art, and its approaches to representation. According to the text, Artistic Data Visualization: Beyond Visual Analytics (2007) by Viegas and Wattenburg data visualization is seen as a means for representing data and information using visual elements such as charts, graphs, maps, etc. But because of its scientific origin, it’s been viewed as a tool for sense-making. (Viegas and Wattenburg 2007) Data art on the other hand is an artistic take on data representation, where data sets are used to express emotions and a message to the audience. (Benzi 2020) But at the core of it, all the artwork is still based on factual setoff data. Through these definitions, we already see the relationship between data visualization and data art they are both visual representations of data. Data visualization takes on more of a scientific and straightforward approach to representation aiming to provide viewers with clarity on the information and data set provided giving them a better understanding. Data art on the other hand provides an artistic representation of the data set with no aim of providing clarity or understanding of the data set but rather to evoke emotions, curiosity, and questions from the audience like any artwork should. But an explanation is usually given alongside the artwork especially if the artwork is not straightforward.

Now that we understand that data art and visualization are representations of data in their own way and with different aims. Let us look at what representation is and its theories, representation is the description or depiction of something or someone (Oxford Languages). In the text Representation (2015) by Jenny Kidd the first chapter looks at the theories of representation. The first is ‘representation and reality’ in this part Kidd argues that language is the tying factor to attaching meaning to real life, that “language creates the concept of reality rather than reality existing before language gives words to it”. ( Kidd 2015: 20) Words therefore that are assigned to objects have no direct link to the object through the norms of society people are taught that certain things are this and do not question that. And these assigned words to objects only have meaning because one can differentiate them from other words because if there was no difference then there is no meaning. (Kidd 2015: 21)

The second one is ‘representation and myth’ here Kidd looks at the concepts of denotation and connotation. Denotation refers to the surface level of something the basis of exactly what it is, and connotation refers to the representation that something specific has been assigned by society for example the denotation of a rose is a plant the connotation is romance or love because that is what it has been associated within society. (Kidd 2015: 23) In this theory of representation signs are assigned or associated with specific meanings on the level of connotation due to the way they are represented, these narratives created which might be false or not then can be described as myths. (Kidd 2015: 24)

The third theory is ‘representation and visual communication’ this theory we look at visual communication such as images that are not easy to understand and can be interpreted in many different ways. Because signs such as images are not as easily distinguishable compared to written or spoken words the meaning behind them is often blurred and momentary. (Kidd 2015: 27) Machin and Mayr however offer a more extensive way in which people can view the connotative meaning of images. Stating that if the image is abstract the more apparent the connotative purpose surrounding it, the context in which an image is shown will sometimes determine whether the communication is meant to be connotative or denotative, and lastly that the connotative meaning will most likely be communicated by the artist in a way that the audience will be able to recognize such as global signifiers that have connotative communication understood and shared by most people. (Machin & Mayr in Kidd 2015: 27)

The last theory we will be looking at is ‘representation as persuasion’ here we look at the specific use of something such as imagery or wording to sell an agenda or convince an audience to believe something over something else. (Kidd 2015: 32) Through these theories of representation we can see some of the strategies of representation used in data art and visualisation. Such as with data visualization data visualization is often very scientific and therefore is more of a denotative form of communication for representation and visual communication. And on the surface of representation and reality data visualization has no meaning without assigned text being ascribed to it we would not know what a bar graph was unless if there was language and words attached to it and it can be differentiated from a pie chart.

Data art also makes use of many of these strategies or representations a major one would be representation and myth because data art has artistic purposes rather than plain scientific data representation the art is more likely to look nothing like the data or can be the real-life artistic depiction of the data. Allowing the audience to create their own meanings around what is being presented. Much with representation and visual communication, the meaning could be denotative or connotative such as the data art being based on a data set about flowers and the data art being a flower. Therefore, allowing the audience to speculate that the data set used could be about a flower but if the art had no correlation to the data set that could mean there is more of a connotative meaning to the work of art and that is where persuasion starts to kick in and the artist uses words to convince the audience of what they are seeing through explanation. (Mahoney 2019)

For my own data art, I applied the majority of the representation strategies mentioned my artwork is very abstract and off the bat from the data set used, and in order for one to understand why I chose the art I had to explain it. Which I employed persuasion and my art has a large amount of connotative communication because the work is abstract and not pleasing to the eye. But I took my own turn on the data art based on my own beliefs and the way I analyse the data and its denotative meaning in real life. And in turn, assigned meaning to my artwork through language and words. Through this essay, we have seen the relationship between data visualisation and data art through their sharing in both being forms or representations the other more scientific and the other with more artistic aim. They are both based on a factual dataset. And the strategies of representation both apply to each of them but in a different manner.
